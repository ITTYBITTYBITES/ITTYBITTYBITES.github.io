## 1. Our Promise

We build things worth returning to.

The purpose of this platform is not simply to create software.

Software is the medium.

The purpose is to create experiences that earn a person's time, attention, and trust.

Every experience within this platform should give people a reason to return.

That reason may be:

- discovering something new,
- solving a challenge,
- learning something useful,
- experiencing a creative idea,
- exploring a story,
- creating something themselves,
- or finding a tool that improves their life.

Return value is the foundation of the platform.

A visitor should never feel that they are merely consuming content. They should feel that they are participating in something that evolves.

## 2. The Platform Exists Beyond Individual Projects

Individual games, applications, articles, tools, and experiments are not isolated products.

They are parts of a larger ecosystem.

The platform exists to allow ideas to grow, connect, and evolve over time.

A successful addition does not only improve itself.

It improves the platform around it.

Every experience should contribute to:

- discovery,
- learning,
- engagement,
- trust,
- and future possibilities.

## 3. The Three Laws

All future decisions are measured against three foundational laws.

**Law 1: Protect the platform before extending it.**

A new capability must not weaken the foundation that supports everything else.

Performance, accessibility, security, maintainability, and user trust are not acceptable trade-offs for short-term gains.

If an addition damages the platform, the correct action is redesign, not implementation.

**Law 2: Every addition must make the entire platform better, not just the feature being built.**

A solution created for one experience should be evaluated for broader usefulness.

Shared problems should receive shared solutions.

The platform should become more capable with every addition.

**Law 3: The repository is always deployable.**

The main branch represents a working product.

Development should never leave the platform in an unstable state.

Every completed implementation must preserve:

- successful builds,
- passing verification,
- deployment capability,
- documentation accuracy,
- and a clear history of changes.

## 4. The Platform Philosophy

The platform is built around a simple idea:

**Small, meaningful interactions create lasting experiences.**

The smallest unit of value is not a page.

It is not a screen.

It is not a feature.

It is an interaction between a person and the platform.

Those interactions combine into experiences.

Experiences combine into collections.

Collections combine into the evolving platform.

## 5. Human-Centered by Default

The platform is designed for people first.

Technology exists to support the experience.

The platform should respect:

- the user's time,
- the user's attention,
- the user's privacy,
- the user's abilities,
- and the user's choices.

The platform should be welcoming to curious newcomers while remaining powerful for experienced users.

## 6. Evolution Over Replacement

The platform should evolve continuously.

Change should be intentional.

Legacy systems should not be dismissed simply because they are old. They contain lessons, patterns, and history.

The goal is not to erase the past.

The goal is to learn from it and build something better.
