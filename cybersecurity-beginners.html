Cybersecurity for Beginners: 20 Threats Everyone Should Know in 2026

Last updated: June 2026 | By Itty Bitty Bites

You do not need to be a hacker to understand cybersecurity. You need to understand the 20 most common ways attackers target ordinary people -- and the simple defenses that stop 95% of attacks.

Password and Authentication Threats

1. Password Spraying

Trying common passwords (Password123, Welcome1) across thousands of accounts. Defense: use unique passwords 12+ characters with a password manager.

2. Credential Stuffing

Using leaked passwords from one breach to access other accounts. Defense: never reuse passwords across sites.

3. Brute Force

Trying every possible combination until the password is found. Defense: long passwords and account lockout after failed attempts.

Social Engineering Threats

4. Phishing

Fake emails mimicking trusted senders. Full guide here.

5. Smishing

Phishing via SMS text messages. "Your package is delayed, click here." Defense: never click links in unexpected texts.

6. Vishing

Phone-based social engineering. "This is your bank's fraud department." Defense: hang up and call the bank's real number.

7. Pretexting

Creating a fake scenario to extract information. Defense: verify identity through separate channels.

Malware Threats

8. Ransomware

Encrypts your files and demands payment. Defense: regular backups on disconnected drives.

9. Trojans

Malware disguised as legitimate software. Defense: only download from official sources.

10. Keyloggers

Records every keystroke including passwords. Defense: keep antivirus updated, use 2FA.

11. Spyware

Monitors your activity secretly. Defense: regular malware scans, avoid suspicious downloads.

Network Threats

12. Man-in-the-Middle

Attacker intercepts communication between you and a website. Defense: use HTTPS, avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive tasks.

13. DNS Poisoning

Redirecting website addresses to fake sites. Defense: use trusted DNS providers (Cloudflare 1.1.1.1).

14. Evil Twin Wi-Fi

Fake Wi-Fi hotspot mimicking a real one. Defense: verify network names, use VPN on public networks.

Data and Privacy Threats

15. Data Breaches

When companies lose your data. Defense: use unique passwords, monitor haveibeenpwned.com.

16. Identity Theft

Someone uses your personal information fraudulently. Defense: freeze credit, monitor accounts.

17. Doxxing

Publishing private information online. Defense: minimize personal info on social media.

Emerging Threats

18. Deepfake Scams

AI-generated video/audio impersonation. Defense: verify unusual requests through separate channels.

19. IoT Attacks

Hacking smart home devices. Defense: change default passwords, update firmware.

20. Supply Chain Attacks

Compromising software updates from trusted vendors. Defense: keep systems updated but verify unusual update prompts.
