## 1. What is the Library Compass?

Every Collection in ITTYBITTYBITES is guided by a **Library Compass**. 

The Compass is not a technical specification or a feature list. It is a single, focused outcome that answers:
> *"When someone finishes this collection, what should they see differently?"*

By focusing on a conceptual outcome rather than a checklist of tasks, the Library Compass ensures that all interactions serve a higher narrative goal. We build interactive books, not interactive software.

---

## 2. Permanent Citation Scheme

To support future scaling from 30 experiences to 300 and beyond, every experience is reserved a **Permanent Citation Identifier**. This provides a stable, permanent identity that never changes, regardless of future platform updates or visual changes. 

Users can share, discuss, and reference experiences using these concise citations (e.g., *"You should try H-003"* or *"My favorite is N-002"*).

### Identifier Structure:
- `[Prefix]-[Three-Digit Index]` (e.g., `H-001`, `M-003`, `S-005`)

### Prefix Registry:
| Collection | Prefix | Code Example | Range |
|------------|--------|--------------|-------|
| Foundations | `F` | `F-001` through `F-099` | General/Interactions Bedrock |
| History | `H` | `H-001` through `H-099` | Historical Thinking / Evidence |
| Science | `S` | `S-001` through `S-099` | Iterative Discovery / Inquiry |

## 3. The Compass Index

Here is the authoritative mapping of all active and upcoming collections.

### 1. Foundations (`F`)
- **The Compass**: Meaningful interactions are the bedrock of understanding.
- **Editor's Note**: Learning is not a passive pursuit. The moments of highest clarity occur when we interact with an idea directly, test its boundaries, and see how it responds.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 2. History (`H`)
- **The Compass**: Evidence is stronger than certainty.
- **Editor's Note**: History isn't about memorizing dates. It's about learning how evidence changes what we believe happened.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 3. Science (`S`)
- **The Compass**: Good questions change with new evidence.
- **Editor's Note**: Science is not an accumulation of facts; it is a way of asking questions that get progressively more refined.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 4. Nature (`N`)
- **The Compass**: Everything is connected.
- **Editor's Note**: Nature is a nested web of feedback loops, adaptation, and symbiotic relationships. To understand a living system, we must look at how the parts affect the whole.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 5. Creativity (`C`)
- **The Compass**: Constraints can produce originality.
- **Editor's Note**: Infinite choices breed paralysis. True creative freedom does not come from starting with a blank canvas, but from discovering what is possible within a set of rigid boundaries.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 6. Engineering (`E`)
- **The Compass**: Every solution involves trade-offs.
- **Editor's Note**: In the real world, there is no such thing as a perfect design. Every bridge, every feedback loop, and every system optimization is a series of deliberate compromise choices.
- **Status**: Complete (v1.0.0)

### 7. Mathematics (`M`)
- **The Compass**: Math is a language for understanding patterns.
- **Editor's Note**: Mathematics is not about memorizing formulas or completing mechanical calculations. It is a creative framework for recognizing patterns, exploring relationships, predicting possibilities, and discovering the hidden structures that shape our world.
- **Experience Themes**:
  - **Patterns**: Noticing repetition, sequences, rules, prediction, and abstraction.
  - **Estimation**: Developing approximate reasoning through magnitude, scale, and mental models.
  - **Symmetry**: Exploring balance, transformations, repetition, and invariance.
  - **Probability**: Building intuition for chance, expected outcomes, and decisions under uncertainty.
  - **Proof**: Understanding how examples, counterexamples, and logic differ from certainty.
- **Status**: Complete (Release 0.7 / v1.0.0)

### 8. Society & Mind (`X`)
- **The Compass**: Cooperation, cognition, and communication are the bridges that connect human systems.
- **Editor's Note**: Every human system begins with individuals trying to understand the world, each other, and themselves. By exploring how minds perceive, remember, decide, and cooperate, we begin to understand the invisible systems that shape everyday life.
- **Active Themes**:
  - **Attention**: What we notice shapes what we experience.
  - **Bias**: The mind uses shortcuts because it must make decisions quickly.
  - **Memory**: Memory is a reconstruction, not a perfect recording.
  - **Cooperation**: Groups create systems through trust, incentives, and shared rules.
  - **Decision Making**: Choices are shaped by information, uncertainty, and consequences.
- **Status**: Complete (Release 0.8)
