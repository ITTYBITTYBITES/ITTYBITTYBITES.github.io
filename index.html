What is the Stroop Effect?

The Stroop Effect is a psychological phenomenon where your brain struggles when information conflicts. Try reading the COLOR of a word when the word itself spells a different color. Your brain has to override its automatic response -- that's System 2 overriding System 1. The 2-Second Witness is built entirely on this principle.

System 1 vs System 2 Thinking

Nobel Prize winner Daniel Kahneman identified two systems of thinking. System 1 is fast, automatic, and often wrong. System 2 is slow, deliberate, and accurate. Most games test knowledge. The 2-Second Witness tests your ability to override System 1 and engage System 2 under pressure. That's a skill that transfers to real life -- from driving decisions to medical diagnoses.

Why 2 Seconds?

Research shows that most snap judgments happen in under 2 seconds. First impressions, threat assessment, pattern recognition -- your brain makes these calls before you're even conscious of them. Training yourself to be accurate in that 2-second window makes you sharper in every area of life.
