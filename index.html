OPERATION OVERVIEW

OBJECTIVE

A rapid-fire cognitive training game built on the Stroop Effect — the psychological phenomenon where your brain fights itself when information conflicts. Each round: a flash word primes your brain, a scenario appears for 2 seconds, then you identify the truth from four options. Your instincts will betray you. Your assumptions will mislead you. The training teaches you to override System 1 and see clearly under pressure.

PROTOCOL

  1. BRIEFING — context for the scenario.
  2. FLASH — a priming word designed to influence your perception.
  3. WITNESS — critical information appears for 2 seconds.
  4. IDENTIFY — select the correct answer before time expires.

Difficulty scales across 4 clearance levels: RECRUIT (7s witness), AGENT (5s), OPERATIVE (3.5s), GHOST (2.5s). Higher clearance = more XP but greater cognitive interference.

THE SCIENCE

CLASSIFIED

THE STROOP EFFECT

Named after John Ridley Stroop (1935). Your brain processes word meaning faster than color, creating a measurable conflict when the two disagree. This interference reveals the fundamental tension between automatic processing (System 1) and controlled processing (System 2). The 2-Second Witness weaponizes this conflict in every round. Full briefing >>

CLASSIFIED

SYSTEM 1 vs SYSTEM 2

Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman identified two decision systems. System 1 is fast, automatic, and often wrong. System 2 is slow, deliberate, and accurate. Most errors occur when System 1 handles situations requiring System 2. Training under time pressure builds faster System 2 activation. Decision training >>

CLASSIFIED

COGNITIVE PRIMING

Exposure to a stimulus unconsciously influences your response to the next stimulus. The flash word in each round is a deliberate cognitive prime. At RECRUIT level, it helps. At GHOST level, it actively misleads. Training yourself to recognize and override misleading primes is the core skill that transfers to real-world decision making. Priming analysis >>
