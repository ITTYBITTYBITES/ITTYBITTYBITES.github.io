THE SCIENCE

CLASSIFIED

THE STROOP EFFECT

Named after John Ridley Stroop (1935). Your brain processes word meaning faster than color, creating a measurable conflict when the two disagree. This interference reveals the fundamental tension between automatic processing (System 1) and controlled processing (System 2). The 2-Second Witness weaponizes this conflict in every round.

Full briefing
CLASSIFIED

SYSTEM 1 vs SYSTEM 2

Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman identified two decision systems. System 1 is fast, automatic, and often wrong. System 2 is slow, deliberate, and accurate. Most errors occur when System 1 handles situations requiring System 2. Training under time pressure builds faster System 2 activation.

Decision training
CLASSIFIED

COGNITIVE PRIMING

Exposure to a stimulus unconsciously influences your response to the next stimulus. The flash word in each round is a deliberate cognitive prime. At RECRUIT level, it helps. At GHOST level, it actively misleads. Training yourself to recognize and override misleading primes is the core skill that transfers to real-world decision making.
