Food Safety Temperature Guide: What Every Cook Needs to Know

Last updated: June 2026 | By Itty Bitty Bites

The "danger zone" for food is between 40F (4C) and 140F (60C). Bacteria multiply rapidly in this range. Here is everything you need to know to keep food safe.

Safe Cooking Temperatures

  • Poultry (chicken, turkey): 165F (74C)
  • Ground meat: 160F (71C)
  • Beef steaks/roasts: 145F (63C) + 3 min rest
  • Pork: 145F (63C) + 3 min rest
  • Fish: 145F (63C)
  • Eggs: 160F (71C) for dishes
  • Leftovers: Reheat to 165F (74C)

The 2-Hour Rule

Food left in the danger zone for more than 2 hours should be discarded. In temperatures above 90F (32C), this drops to 1 hour. When in doubt, throw it out -- food poisoning is not worth the risk.

Storage Guidelines

  • Refrigerator: 40F (4C) or below
  • Freezer: 0F (-18C) or below
  • Cooked leftovers: Use within 3-4 days
  • Raw poultry: Use within 1-2 days
  • Ground meat: Use within 1-2 days
