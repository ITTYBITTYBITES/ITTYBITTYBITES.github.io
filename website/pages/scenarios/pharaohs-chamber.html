Pharaoh's Chamber Trial

Cognitive Science Framing

Human working memory can typically retain 4±1 discrete visual objects simultaneously. When exposed to a rich environment for only two seconds, your brain prioritizes high-salience features while pruning secondary background detail—a vulnerability explored in forensic psychology.

Memory Illusions FAQ

Why do witnesses miss obvious changes?

Without active focal attention directed specifically toward an object during the critical encoding window, visual changes are frequently wiped during saccadic eye movements.
