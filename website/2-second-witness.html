Overview

Rapid-fire truth under pressure.

A rapid-fire cognitive training game built on the Stroop Effect — the psychological phenomenon where your brain fights itself when information conflicts. Each round: a flash word primes your brain, a scenario appears for 2 seconds, then you identify the truth from four options. Your instincts will lie.

Difficulty scales across 4 clearance levels: RECRUIT (7s), AGENT (5s), OPERATIVE (3.5s), GHOST (2.5s). Higher clearance = more XP but greater cognitive interference.

The science

Cognitive interference as gameplay.

The Stroop Effect

Named after John Ridley Stroop (1935). Your brain processes word meaning faster than color, creating a measurable conflict when the two disagree. This interference reveals the fundamental tension between automatic processing (System 1) and controlled processing (System 2). The 2-Second Witness weaponizes this conflict into training.

System 1 vs System 2

Nobel laureate Daniel Kahneman identified two decision systems. System 1 is fast, automatic, and often wrong. System 2 is slow, deliberate, and accurate. Most errors occur when System 1 handles situations requiring System 2. Training under time pressure builds faster System 2 activation.

Cognitive Priming

Exposure to a stimulus unconsciously influences your response to the next stimulus. The flash word in each round is a deliberate cognitive prime. At RECRUIT level, it helps. At GHOST level, it actively misleads. Training yourself to recognize and override misleading primes is the core skill that transfers beyond the game.
