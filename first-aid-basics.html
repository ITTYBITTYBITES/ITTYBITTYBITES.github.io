Basic First Aid Everyone Should Know: 12 Life-Saving Skills

Last updated: June 2026 | By Itty Bitty Bites

These 12 skills could save a life. Learn them before you need them.

1. CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

If someone is unresponsive and not breathing normally: Call 911. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest at 100-120 compressions per minute (tempo of "Stayin' Alive" by the Bee Gees). Push at least 2 inches deep. Do not stop until help arrives.

2. Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)

Stand behind the person. Make a fist above their navel. Grasp with other hand. Give quick upward thrusts until the object dislodges.

3. Severe Bleeding

Apply firm, direct pressure with a clean cloth. Do NOT remove the cloth if blood soaks through -- add more on top. Elevate the wound above the heart if possible.

4. Burns

Cool with running water for 10-20 minutes. Do NOT use ice, butter, or toothpaste. Cover with a clean, non-fluffy dressing.

5. Shock

Signs: pale skin, rapid pulse, confusion. Lay the person down, elevate legs 12 inches, keep warm, do NOT give food or drink.

6. Heart Attack Signs

Chest pressure, pain radiating to arm/jaw, shortness of breath, cold sweat. Call 911 immediately. Have them chew an aspirin if not allergic.

7. Stroke Signs (FAST)

Face drooping, Arm weakness, Speech difficulty, Time to call 911.

8. Allergic Reaction (Anaphylaxis)

If they have an EpiPen: inject into outer thigh through clothing. Call 911 even if symptoms improve.

9. Fractures

Immobilize the injured area. Do NOT try to realign the bone. Apply ice wrapped in cloth. Seek medical attention.

10. Heat Stroke

Hot, red, dry skin. No sweating. Confusion. Cool rapidly with cold water, ice packs to neck/armpits/groin. Call 911.

11. Hypothermia

Shivering, confusion, drowsiness. Move to warmth. Remove wet clothing. Warm gradually with blankets. Give warm (not hot) drinks if conscious.

12. Recovery Position

For unconscious but breathing people: roll onto their side, tilt head back, top knee forward for stability. Monitor breathing.
