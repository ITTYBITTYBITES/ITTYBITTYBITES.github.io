The Dunning-Kruger Effect: Why Incompetent People Think They're Experts

Last updated: June 2026 | By Itty Bitty Bites

The skills needed to produce correct responses are the SAME skills needed to recognize correct responses. If you lack the skill, you cannot recognize that you lack it.

The Four Stages

  1. Unconscious Incompetence: Peak overconfidence. "How hard can it be?"
  2. Conscious Incompetence: "There is SO much more to this than I thought."
  3. Conscious Competence: You can do it with effort. Confidence rebuilds on solid ground.
  4. Unconscious Competence: True expertise. Paradoxically, confidence often lower than a beginner's.

Why It Matters

This affects everyone in domains where they lack expertise. A brilliant surgeon may overestimate their investing ability. Expertise does not transfer between domains.

How to Counteract It

  • Seek external feedback
  • Study broadly to calibrate knowledge
  • Track predictions vs outcomes
  • Practice metacognition
