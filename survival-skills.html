10 Survival Skills Everyone Should Know (Science-Based Guide)

Last updated: June 2026 | By Itty Bitty Bites

Most survival myths will get you killed. Here are 10 skills that actually work.

1. Water

You survive 3 days without it. Priority: flowing streams (boiled 1 min), rainwater, dew collection. Avoid: eating snow directly (lowers core temp).

2. Shelter

Hypothermia kills faster than anything. Build small debris shelters. Avoid hilltops (wind) and valleys (cold pools).

3. Fire

Carry waterproof matches AND a ferro rod. Tinder prep is 90% of success. Best: birch bark, cedar fibers, pine resin.

4. Navigation

Day: stick shadow method. Night: North Star via Big Dipper pointer stars. Myth: moss does NOT reliably grow on north side.

5. Signaling

Three of anything = distress. Signal mirror visible 10 miles. At night, fire is best signal.

6. Food

You survive 3 weeks without it. Do NOT waste energy hunting. Focus: insects (high protein), known plants only, passive fishing.

7. First Aid

Stop bleeding (direct pressure). Treat shock (elevate legs). Immobilize fractures. Full guide here.

8. Weather Reading

Red morning = rain coming. Dropping pressure = storm. Vertical cumulus = thunderstorms.

9. Cold Survival

C.O.L.D.: Clean clothes, avoid Overheating, Layers, stay Dry. Wet clothing loses 90% insulation.

10. Mental Resilience

Psychological resilience is the #1 survival predictor -- more than fitness or gear. This is trainable.
